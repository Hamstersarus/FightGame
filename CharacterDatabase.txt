FIGHT — CHARACTER DATABASE

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GOBLIN  👹
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           50
Attack Power: 12
Symbol:       👹

Description:
  Goblins are scrappy and get more dangerous as a fight drags on. What they
  lack in raw power they make up for with relentless aggression — the longer
  they survive, the harder they hit.

Special Ability — Frenzy:
  The Goblin's attack power increases by +2 for every turn it survives.
  Turn 1: 12 damage. Turn 2: 14. Turn 3: 16. And so on, with no cap.

Behavior Notes:
  - Starts weak; relies on outlasting the opponent long enough for Frenzy
    to bring its damage up to a threatening level
  - Fragile — most opponents can finish it in 3-4 hits before Frenzy pays off
  - Best outcome is a slow opponent (Troll, Zombie, Knight) who can't end
    the fight before Frenzy scales up

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `int turnsAlive` field, initialized to 0
  - Increment `turnsAlive` at the start of each turn
  - Effective attack = `attackPower + (turnsAlive * 2)`
  - Apply this modifier before damage is subtracted from target's HP

WITCH  🧙
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           65
Attack Power: 25
Symbol:       🧙

Description:
  The Witch deals devastating magic damage but cannot take many hits. She is
  a high-risk, high-reward character who wins fights quickly or not at all.

Special Ability — Hex:
  Every 3rd attack, the Witch casts a Hex on the opponent. A Hexed opponent
  loses 5 HP at the start of each of their turns for 3 turns (15 total
  damage over time).

Behavior Notes:
  - Glass cannon — wins fast or loses fast
  - Hex stacks are dangerous: if the opponent is hexed and still alive,
    the DoT continues to chip away even when the Witch is not attacking
  - Vulnerable to Ninja (First Strike can end her before she attacks)
    and Dragon (Inferno burns through her low HP)

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `int attackCount` field; increment each time `attack()` is called
  - When `attackCount % 3 == 0`, apply Hex to opponent
  - Hex requires status fields on `Character`: `int hexTurnsRemaining`,
    `int hexDamagePerTurn = 5`
  - Tick Hex damage at the start of the hexed character's turn


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VAMPIRE  🧛
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           90
Attack Power: 18
Symbol:       🧛

Description:
  The Vampire is a durable fighter who heals from every attack, making them
  harder to kill the longer a fight goes on. Best in prolonged 1v1 battles.

Special Ability — Bat Form:
  Every 5th turn, the Vampire transforms into a bat for 1 turn. While in
  bat form it is completely immune to the opponent's attack (0 damage) and
  skips its own attack that turn. It reverts to Vampire form automatically
  the following turn.

Behavior Notes:
  - Bat Form triggers on turns 5, 10, 15, 20... making the Vampire's
    survival rhythm predictable but reliable — the opponent effectively
    loses one attack every 5 turns
  - Most valuable against opponents who deal high damage per hit (Dragon,
    Ninja, Witch) where absorbing a single incoming hit can be the
    difference between surviving and dying
  - Less impactful against low-attack opponents (Goblin, Troll, Zombie)
    where the damage avoided is small relative to the attack also skipped
  - Countered by DoT effects (Hex, Burn) which tick between turns and
    are not blocked by Bat Form immunity

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `int turnCount` field; increment at the start of each turn
  - When `turnCount % 5 == 0`, set `boolean inBatForm = true`
  - During damage resolution: if `inBatForm`, set incoming damage to 0
    and skip the Vampire's attack for that turn
  - Set `inBatForm = false` at the end of the bat-form turn
  - Print a message on transformation:
    "🧛 transforms into a bat! 🦇 The attack passes right through!"


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NINJA  🥷
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           55
Attack Power: 30
Symbol:       🥷

Description:
  The Ninja has the highest attack power in the game but the second-lowest
  HP. Built to eliminate the opponent before they can fight back.

Special Ability — First Strike:
  The Ninja always attacks before the opponent when a turn begins, regardless
  of normal turn order. If the opponent is defeated by First Strike, the
  opponent does not get to retaliate that turn.

Behavior Notes:
  - Most effective against high-HP opponents who can be severely weakened
    or one-shot before they retaliate
  - Extremely fragile — will fall quickly to anyone who survives the
    first hit
  - Countered by Dragon and Troll, who have enough HP to absorb the
    opening strike and hit back hard

Implementation Notes:
  - Before normal turn resolution, check if the player or opponent is a Ninja
  - If so, the Ninja's `attack()` resolves first
  - Call `isAlive()` on the target before allowing the opponent to retaliate

KNIGHT  💂‍♀️
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           120
Attack Power: 15
Symbol:       💂‍♀️

Description:
  The Knight is a disciplined fighter who uses their shield to absorb
  punishment. High HP and reliable damage, with a defensive ability that
  makes burst damage less effective against them.

Special Ability — Parry:
  Every 3rd incoming attack is fully blocked — the Knight takes 0 damage
  from that hit. The counter resets after each successful parry.

Behavior Notes:
  - Strong against opponents who rely on consistent damage output
    (Vampire, Goblin, Troll, Zombie) where the free blocked hit
    compounds over a long fight
  - Less effective against opponents with burst or DoT abilities
    (Dragon's Inferno, Witch's Hex) since Parry only negates direct hits
  - A patient, reliable pick — rarely dominant but rarely loses badly

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `int incomingAttackCount` field; increment each time the Knight
    is targeted by an attack
  - When `incomingAttackCount % 3 == 0`, set incoming damage to 0
    for that hit
  - Apply the check before subtracting damage from `hp`


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DRAGON  🐉
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           150
Attack Power: 35
Symbol:       🐉

Description:
  The Dragon is the most powerful character in the game. Near-impossible to
  take down quickly and capable of eliminating most opponents in 2-3 hits.

Special Ability — Inferno:
  Every 4th attack, the Dragon breathes fire, dealing 2× attack power
  (70 damage) and applying Burn to the opponent: 8 HP lost per turn
  for 3 turns.

Special Ability — Fire Shot 🔥:
  The Dragon can choose to shoot a bolt of fire at the opponent from
  anywhere on the board. Deals 30 damage and ignores distance entirely —
  the Dragon can fire from across the map. Has a 3-turn cooldown after
  each use.

Behavior Notes:
  - Wins nearly every 1v1 matchup on stats alone
  - Burn from Inferno can finish off low-HP opponents (Goblin, Witch,
    Ninja) without needing a direct killing blow
  - Fire Shot gives the Dragon a reliable ranged option — useful when
    the opponent is retreating or hasn't closed the distance yet
  - Fire Shot + Inferno on back-to-back turns is the Dragon's most
    devastating combo: 30 damage then 70 + Burn in quick succession
  - The only realistic counters are Alien (Cattle Drop burst) and
    Zombie (Undying forces an extra round)

Implementation Notes:
  - Inferno: add `int attackCount` field; trigger when `attackCount % 4 == 0`
  - Inferno damage = `attackPower * 2` (70)
  - Burn requires status fields on `Character`: `int burnTurnsRemaining`,
    `int burnDamagePerTurn = 8`; apply at start of burned character's turn
  - Fire Shot: add `int fireShotCooldown` field, initialized to 0
  - Fire Shot appears as a choosable action in the combat menu only when
    `fireShotCooldown == 0`
  - On use: deal 30 damage, set `fireShotCooldown = 3`
  - Decrement `fireShotCooldown` by 1 at the end of each turn if > 0


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ZOMBIE  🧟
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           100
Attack Power: 10
Symbol:       🧟

Description:
  The Zombie refuses to die. Its attack is the weakest in the game, but a
  second-chance mechanic means finishing it off requires two kill shots
  instead of one.

Special Ability — Undying:
  The first time the Zombie would be reduced to 0 HP or below, it instead
  survives with 1 HP. This can only trigger once per battle.

Behavior Notes:
  - Forces every opponent to land one extra killing blow, buying an
    additional turn to deal damage
  - Poor performance against DoT effects (Hex, Burn) because Undying
    does not protect against damage-over-time ticks — a hexed or
    burning Zombie can die between turns without Undying triggering
  - Wins only through sheer endurance; does not have a realistic path
    to victory against Dragon or Ninja

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `boolean hasUndying = true` field
  - In the damage resolution step: if `hp` would go `<= 0` and
    `hasUndying` is true, set `hp = 1` and `hasUndying = false`
  - Print a revival message when Undying triggers
  - DoT ticks are applied separately and bypass this check

ALIEN  👽
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           70
Attack Power: 20
Symbol:       👽

Description:
  The Alien's behavior is erratic and hard to plan against. Balanced stats
  make it a reliable pick, but its special ability introduces a dramatic
  swing that can reverse a losing fight in an instant.

Special Ability — Cattle Drop:
  Every 4th attack, the Alien forgoes its normal strike and summons a UFO
  that drops 3 cows on the opponent. Each cow deals 15 damage for a total
  of 45 damage — more than double the Alien's normal attack.

Behavior Notes:
  - Cattle Drop hits harder than any standard attack in the game except
    Dragon's Inferno, making it a reliable burst window every 4 turns
  - The 3 turns between Cattle Drops are spent chipping away at 20 damage
    each; the rhythm is: 20 / 20 / 20 / 45 / 20 / 20 / 20 / 45...
  - Strong against high-HP opponents (Dragon, Troll, Knight) where the
    burst damage matters; less impactful against already-fragile opponents
    who may be finished before the 4th turn arrives
  - Countered by Ninja (First Strike can eliminate the Alien before the
    first Cattle Drop triggers on turn 4)

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `int attackCount` field; increment each time the Alien attacks
  - When `attackCount % 4 == 0`, skip normal damage and apply 45 directly
    to `target.hp`
  - Print a flavored message on Cattle Drop:
    "👽 summons a UFO! 🛸 Three cows 🐄🐄🐄 rain down on <target>! (45 damage)"


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TROLL  🧌
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           130
Attack Power: 12
Symbol:       🧌

Description:
  The Troll is the hardest character to kill through sustained damage alone.
  Its attack output is low, but it regenerates health every turn, making it
  nearly impossible to whittle down.

Special Ability — Regeneration:
  At the end of every turn, the Troll heals 8 HP (up to its max HP).

Behavior Notes:
  - Wins any endurance fight where incoming damage per turn is close to
    or less than 8 HP — Regeneration effectively nullifies weak attackers
  - Countered by high-burst opponents (Dragon, Ninja) who can deal damage
    faster than Regeneration can offset
  - DoT effects (Hex, Burn) tick after Regeneration, making them
    efficient tools for bypassing the healing

Implementation Notes:
  - Store `int maxHp` as a field, set at construction time
  - At the end of each turn (after both attacks resolve), add 8 to the
    Troll's `hp`, then cap at `maxHp`

COMPUTER VIRUS  👾
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HP:           60
Attack Power: 28
Symbol:       👾

Description:
  The Computer Virus corrupts the opponent with escalating damage, dealing
  more harm every consecutive attack. Fragile but terrifying if the fight
  goes on for more than a few turns.

Special Ability — Corrupt:
  Each consecutive attack on the opponent adds +4 to the damage dealt
  (stacks indefinitely, no reset since there is only one opponent).
  Attack 1: 28 dmg. Attack 2: 32. Attack 3: 36. Attack 4: 40. And so on.

Behavior Notes:
  - Starts below average in damage but overtakes most opponents by turn 4-5
  - Fragile — needs to survive long enough for Corrupt to ramp up
  - Devastating against high-HP opponents (Dragon, Troll) when allowed to
    stack: by turn 6-7 it is dealing 52-56 damage per hit
  - Countered by Ninja (First Strike can end it before stacks build) and
    Dragon (raw HP and Inferno outpace early Corrupt stacks)

Implementation Notes:
  - Add `int corruptStacks` field, initialized to 0
  - Before calculating damage: increment `corruptStacks` each attack
  - Final damage = `attackPower + (corruptStacks * 4)`
  - No target-switching logic needed — there is only one opponent
